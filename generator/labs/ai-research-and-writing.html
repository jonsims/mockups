AI for Research & Writing

Crafting an Expansive and Equitable Future for Written Expression with AI

Updated 2026-07-13 Contact: Kristi Girdharry (kgirdharry@babson.edu)

The AI for Research & Writing Lab's work spans curriculum Innovation, scholarly Research, faculty Development & Campus Programming, student Engagement & Experiential Learning, ethical AI & Critical Literacy, and interdisciplinary Collaboration.

  • How do we establish writing processes that utilize generative AI in support of learning rather than replacing it?
  • In what ways can AI augment human creativity, voice, and rhetorical effectiveness across academic, business, and public communication?
  • How do we design AI-integrated writing experiences that are ethical, equitable, and inclusive of diverse linguistic and cultural practices?

Initiatives

Writing and AI Symposium Series

An invitational symposium, run twice so far, convening writing faculty from over 40 southern New England institutions to explore generative AI in writing instruction, literacy, and pedagogy — participatory, values-driven, and focused on community building and collaborative leadership.

"Writing with Robots" course

An undergraduate course where students write with and about AI to reflect on authorship, ethics, and digital identity — genre-based experiments plus group research projects on AI ethics, modeling curiosity, critique, and (sometimes) joy in the face of technological change.
