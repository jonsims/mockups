Work Futures

Researching and Designing New Ways to Organize Work in the Age of AI

Updated 2026-07-13 Contact: Sebastian Fixson (sfixson@babson.edu)

The Work Futures Lab's work spans new AI-courses, cutting-edge industry research, pedagogical innovation, groundbreaking scholarly research, and ethical counsel for programs and faculty.

  • What forms of collaborative work between humans and AI are possible and desirable?
  • What human skills and competences will new forms of work require?
  • How do we support companies and entrepreneurs to develop forms of human/AI collaboration that are beneficial to all stakeholders?

Get Involved

Students: three internship tracks (research, design futuring, communications). Companies and entrepreneurs: partner with the lab on human/AI collaboration research.

Open Student Roles

Generator Work Futures AI Research Intern

Will support the design and execution of research projects on the effects of AI on tasks, jobs, careers, skills, worker satisfaction and well-being.

Generator Work Futures AI Design Intern

Will support design futuring projects to explore possible futures of work, and how to make them a reality.

Generator Work Futures AI Communications Intern

Will support the lab with communications, primarily on social media and professional networks.
