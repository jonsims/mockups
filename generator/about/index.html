About The Generator

We empower innovators to explore, teach, and research the transformative potential of AI in education, business, and society.

The Generator is Babson College's interdisciplinary artificial intelligence lab, part of the C. Dean Metropoulos Institute for Technology and Entrepreneurship. Faculty, students, and partners explore what AI makes possible — and what it puts at risk — in education, business, and society, through eight Specialty Labs (AI Entrepreneurship, Ethics & Society, Work Futures, Experiential Learning, Prototyping, AI/ML Empowerment, Research & Writing, Arts & Performance), a public calendar of events, and a family of standing programs. Everyone is welcome — enthusiasts, skeptics, and the simply curious; no AI experience required. Not sure where to start? Find your lab →

Interdisciplinary Thinking

At The Generator, we believe that the future of AI is not just in the hands of computer scientists — but in the collaborative efforts of experts across diverse fields. Unlike traditional AI labs, which often operate in silos, The Generator is a melting pot of action and ideas where entrepreneurship meets art and design, and technology crosscuts with the humanities. Our experience has shown that this convergence of diverse expertise leads to solutions that are more creative, inclusive, and effective for innovators of all kinds. Here, interdisciplinary collaboration is not just an option — it's the bedrock of our approach.

Entrepreneurial AI

As the #1 school for Entrepreneurship and Entrepreneurial Leadership, Babson is uniquely positioned to seize upon the emerging AI-age. AI is the single most important technological, societal, ethical, and business innovation issue of our time. The Generator unites Babson's faculty, students, and alumni, as well as Babson's industry partners and global collaborators — through the Babson Regional AI Network (B.R.A.I.N.), the AI Innovators Bootcamp training 1,000 small business owners, and AI Credits that fund student ventures — to understand entrepreneurial opportunities and risks in the AI landscape.

Who Runs It

The Generator is led by an interdisciplinary Babson faculty team — each Specialty Lab page introduces its leaders, from founding director Erik Noyes to the student Explorers who co-lead the lab's programming and organize the Buildathons. Press and research coverage is collected on the News page — including WCVB's visit to the lab.
